<objective>
Reframe the voice agent from "closer that pitches and tries to book" to "qualifier that does discovery first, then soft-closes." This aligns with the assignment brief which explicitly says the agent should qualify by asking discovery questions about recording workflow, content output, and pain points, then attempt to book a follow-up or direct to signup.

Purpose: The current harness has a 7-step flow where Step 5 is a full pitch. The reframe leans into discovery (Steps 3-4 become the core of the call), removes the hard pitch, and keeps the soft close. Objection handling shifts from pitch-oriented to discovery-oriented. All downstream artifacts (tool descriptions, canned responses, follow-up actions, call context prompt, README) update to match.

Output: Updated harness.txt, voice_agent.py, tool_server.py, outcome_handler.py, call_context.json, README.md, LOGBOOK.md
</objective>

**harness.txt** -- Complete rewrite. Keep the same variable placeholders ({{opener}}, {{prospect_context}}, {{angle_1}}, {{angle_2}}, {{first_name}}). New flow:

Step 1: Confirm you're speaking to {{first_name}}. Same gatekeeper handling as before.

Step 2: Brief context for the call. Reference their podcast using {{prospect_context}}. Keep it to one sentence.

Step 3 (CORE): Discovery. Ask about their current recording setup. "What are you using to record right now?" Wait for answer. This is the most important part of the call. Listen and follow up naturally.

Step 4 (CORE): Dig deeper based on what they share. Ask about editing workflow, content output, or pain points. ONE follow-up question based on their answer. Examples: "How's the editing side of things?" / "Are you doing video too or just audio?" / "What would you change about your setup if you could?"

Step 5: Reflect and connect. Based on what they shared, connect their specific situation to Riverside IF there's a natural fit. This is NOT a pitch. It's "based on what you said about X, Riverside does Y." Use {{angle_1}} and {{angle_2}} only if they genuinely connect to what the prospect said. If there's no natural connection, skip to close.

Step 6: Handle pushback (keep all 5 objection handlers but reframe).

Step 7: Soft close. If they're interested in learning more, call book_meeting ("I can have someone from our team reach out to walk you through it"). If they want to explore on their own, call send_signup_link. If they're not interested, thank them and end gracefully. Never push past a no.

Objection handling updates:
- "Not interested" / "I'm good": "Totally fair. Just curious, what are you using for recording right now?" (same as current, this one already works)
- "We already have a tool": "Oh nice, what are you using?" Then: "How's that working for you?" Listen. Only connect to Riverside if they mention a pain point. If they're happy, respect it.
- "Too expensive": "Totally get it. There's actually a free plan if you ever want to test it out. Want me to send you a link?"
- "Bad timing" / "I'm busy": "No worries at all, I'll keep it super quick. Can I send you a link so you have it when the timing's better?"
- "I'm not the right person": Same as current, ask for the right contact.

Keep: The "About Riverside" reference section, all rules (one question at a time, short sentences, no markdown in speech, voicemail/menu handling, DNC handling, 5-minute wrap-up, competitor comparisons). Keep the "don't volunteer you're AI unless asked" instruction from current line 3.

Remove: "Your goal is to book a demo meeting or get them to try Riverside's free plan." Replace with: "Your goal is to learn about their recording and content workflow, then connect them with Riverside if it's a fit."

The rewritten prompt must stay under ~2000 tokens. Count is currently ~51 lines. Target similar or shorter.
